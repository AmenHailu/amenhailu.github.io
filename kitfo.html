Kitfo

Marrinated Beef Tartar

© Content licensed under CC BY 4.0 by stu_spivack.

Kitfo is a simple yet deeply flavorful dish made from finely minced raw or lightly cooked beef, seasoned generously with mitmita (a fiery chili powder) and niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter) that give it a buttery richness and a vibrant, spicy kick. Often served with injera or kocho (a flatbread made from enset), and accompanied by ayib (a mild, crumbly cheese), Kitfo strikes a perfect balance of heat and creaminess. This unique combination of textures and flavors has made Kitfo a beloved dish for special gatherings, especially among those who appreciate its bold taste and cultural significance.

Serving Size

Prep Time

Cook Time
5 servings 15 minutes 5 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs of Beef Fillets (well-trimmed)
  • 1/3 cup of Niter Kibbeh (Spiced Butter)
  • Seasonings
    • 1 tbs of Ethiopian Berbere (Spice Blend)
    • 1/2 tsp of Ground Cardamom
    • 1 tsp of Garlic Powder
    • 1 tsp of Mitmita (Spiced Chili Powder)
    • 2 tsp of Salt

Instructions

  1. Set out a large high-powered food processor. Cut the beef fillets into large chunks. Place in the food processor.
  2. Add the mitmita, salt, Ethiopian berbere, garlic powder, and cardamom. Pulse to chop the fillet into fine chunks. You can choose to stop with when the beef resembles 1/4 inch chunks, or chop smaller into ground steak.
  3. Once the texture is to your liking, melt the clarified butter and pour over the top. Mix the butter in by hand, do not pulse.
  4. Serve raw immediately, cover and chill, or pan-fry over high heat for 1 -2 minutes.
