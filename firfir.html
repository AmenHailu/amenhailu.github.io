Firfir

Spiced Shredded Injera

Firfir is a comforting Ethiopian dish that combines torn pieces of injera (the iconic Ethiopian flatbread made from teff flour) with a rich mix of niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter) and berbere, a vibrant spice blend. The torn injera soaks up these spices, resulting in a warm, tangy, and boldly flavored dish with a uniquely soft, chewy texture. Often enjoyed for breakfast or brunch, Firfir is sometimes paired with additional ingredients like tomatoes, onions, or bell peppers to add a fresh, bright contrast to the deep, spiced flavors. It’s traditionally served with yogurt or ayib (a mild Ethiopian cheese) on the side, providing a cooling balance that enhances the overall experience. Firfir is a popular way to use leftover injera, making it both a practical and delicious part of Ethiopian cuisine.

Serving Size Prep Time Cook Time
5 servings 30 minutes 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 of Injera
  • 3 oz of Tomato Paste
  • Vegetables
    • 2 Chopped Tomatoes
    • 1/2 cup of Water
    • 1/4 cup of Canola Oil
    • 2 Chopped Onions
    • 2 tsp of Minced Ginger
    • 4 Cloves of Minced Garlic
    • 1 De-seeded and Thinly Cut Jalapeño Pepper *Optional*
  • 1 1/2 tbs of Ethiopian Berbere (Spice Blend)
  • 1 tbs of Salt

Instructions

  1. Start by cooking the chopped onions for some time – while stirring – until they begin softening and turning translucent.
  2. Add the canola oil, and continue cooking for a bit until the oil gets hot. Then add the berbere and continue to stir for a few more minutes.
  3. Add the tomato paste to the mix and continue cooking for a while. Then lower the heat to low-medium heat and add the minced ginger and garlic, chopped tomatoes, and some salt, and continue cooking until the tomatoes are cooked well.
  4. Add water and bring to simmer so that it looks like a thick tomato soup. Then shred the injera into smaller pieces and start adding it to the mix.
  5. Continue cooking and stirring gently so that the injera is soaked through and absorbs all of the moisture.
  6. Finally, transfer the Firfir to a serving plate and add the jalapeño peppers on top.
